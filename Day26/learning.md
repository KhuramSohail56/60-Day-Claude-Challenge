:💡 Core Learnings from Day 26
Building and interacting with the Prior Authorization (PA) Workflow Simulator provided deep technical and operational insights into healthcare automation:

US Healthcare Operations & Bottlenecks: Learnt how critical the Prior Authorization process is in the US healthcare system. Incomplete documentation or failing to meet strict medical necessity criteria (like failed conservative management or specific diagnostic validation) acts as a primary administrative bottleneck, causing care delays.

Dynamic State Management in Vanilla JavaScript: Implemented a robust interactive state machine entirely in clean JavaScript to manage real-time transitions across multi-lane architectural boards (Patient Intake → Healthcare Provider → Insurance Payer).

Asynchronous UX Control Flow: Gained practical experience handling asynchronous feedback validation rules, simulating complex clinical pathways such as automatic administrative holds (Pended requests), claim rejections (Denials), and direct downstream resolution strategies like Peer-to-Peer physician consultations.

Accessibility & Device Optimization: Refactored workflow trigger paradigms from strict cursor drag-and-drop mechanisms to click-to-advance structures, optimizing the dashboard interface seamlessly for standard laptop touchpads and responsive mobile viewports.
